## What It Is

Restoration AI is a 24/7 AI receptionist, dispatcher, and front-office automation platform. It answers every call, qualifies every lead, dispatches technicians, sends work authorizations, and follows up after the job — without a human in the loop.

**Built for restoration. Works for any on-site service business that lives and dies by the phone call.**

The product was built specifically for the property restoration industry (water, fire, mold) because restoration has the highest stakes for missed calls — a single unanswered call can mean a $25,000–$100,000 job going to a competitor. But the platform is fully customizable and has been successfully deployed in adjacent industries including duct cleaning, carpet cleaning, HVAC, and other on-site assessment businesses.

## Who Uses It

### Primary ICP: Restoration Contractors
Water damage, fire/smoke, mold remediation, biohazard cleanup, reconstruction. These companies operate 24/7 by definition — emergencies don't wait for business hours. They lose jobs not because of bad pricing or bad work, but because they didn't answer the phone first.

### Expanded ICP: Any Emergency or On-Site Service Business
The platform works for any business where:
- The job requires a technician to visit in person
- The first company to answer typically gets the job
- Call volume is hard to predict (storm surge, seasonal spikes)
- The owner or techs are always in the field and can't be at a desk

**Proven adjacent verticals:**
- Duct cleaning (case study below)
- Carpet cleaning
- HVAC emergency service
- Plumbing
- Roofing (emergency tarping, storm damage)
- Biohazard / crime scene cleanup
- Pest control
- Appliance repair

### The Bigger Picture
Many restoration companies also run secondary businesses. A restoration contractor might also own a duct cleaning company, a carpet cleaning route, or a general contracting division. Restoration AI can serve all of these under one account. This is a key differentiator vs. industry-specific competitors.

## Case Study: Duct Cleaning Company

A company that runs a restoration business also operates a separate duct cleaning company. They asked to try Restoration AI for the duct cleaning side — not restoration.

**Result:** The AI has been booking duct cleaning appointments all day long.

**Why it works:** The AI is designed to scope the situation and recommend an on-site assessment whenever uncertainty exists. For duct cleaning, that means: "Based on what you've described, it sounds like we should send a technician to assess your system. Can I get you scheduled?" That close works for nearly every on-site service category.

**Lesson:** The Restoration AI platform is not a restoration-only tool. It is an on-site service dispatch AI that happens to have been built and battle-tested in the highest-stakes vertical first.

## Custom Services & Pricing (Critical Feature — Often Underrepresented)

Users can configure the AI with any services they provide, including fully custom pricing structures. This is what makes the platform work across industries.

**Example: Carpet Cleaning**
A user adds "Carpet Cleaning" as a service and configures pricing with multiple line items:
- Price per room
- Price per stair step
- Price per couch
- Price per drape
- Price per [any custom item they add]

The AI has complete context. When a customer calls asking about carpet cleaning, the AI can scope the job, provide pricing estimates, and book an appointment or schedule an in-home assessment — all without a human.

**Other customization options:**
- Define which services you DO and DO NOT provide (prevents out-of-scope calls from being treated as leads)
- Add business-specific knowledge (service area zip codes, insurance partner info, certifications, etc.)
- Multiple phone numbers with unique routing rules — e.g., a dedicated Google Ads line vs. the main office line, each with different AI behavior

## The 8 Core Features

### 1. 24/7 AI Receptionist & Dispatch
The AI answers every call within seconds, gathers all necessary job details, and notifies the right technician immediately.

**What it collects on every call:**
- Caller name and property address
- Loss type / service needed
- Urgency level
- Source of damage (water: supply line, appliance, sewage, etc.)
- Affected areas and approximate scope
- Insurance carrier and claim status (if applicable)
- Homeowner authorization to proceed

**Dispatch mechanics:**
- Techs receive instant push notifications
- Accept or decline with a single tap
- 24/7 Cascade Dispatch: sequentially contacts team members in priority order until someone accepts
- Role-based routing: dispatch to Mitigation PMs, Crew Chiefs, Assistant Techs, or Estimators specifically
- Multi-tier dispatch: dispatch a PM immediately, automatically queue a crew for later arrival

---

### 2. Rapid Disqualification
Identifies bad leads within seconds of the call and ends it gracefully — protecting ad spend and keeping the CRM clean.

**What it catches:**
- Out-of-area callers
- Old/pre-existing damage (not covered by insurance)
- Tenants without authority to authorize work
- Solicitors and spam calls
- Jobs outside the company's defined service scope

**Why this matters for paid ads:** Companies running Google LSA or pay-per-lead services get charged for every call, including bad ones. Rapid Disqualification creates documented proof to dispute charges with Google or lead brokers.

Every disqualified call is auto-tagged with a reason and logged in the dashboard.

---

### 3. Smart Route Booking
Books new jobs near where technicians already are, reducing windshield time and enabling more jobs per day.

- Checks a tech's existing appointment schedule before assigning
- Prioritizes proximity to minimize drive time
- If a tech calls in sick, the AI reroutes automatically

---

### 4. Visual Intake
During or after the call, the AI sends the customer an SMS link to upload photos of the damage before the technician leaves.

**The customer flow:**
1. Customer receives lock screen SMS notification from Restoration AI
2. They tap through to a mobile browser form (no app download required)
3. Prompt: "Point your camera at the affected area so we can dispatch the right equipment."
4. They take and submit a photo
5. An AI scan analyzes the image
6. Photos are instantly attached to the job record in the dashboard

**Business impact:**
- Techs arrive knowing what they're walking into
- Correct equipment loaded before departure
- Faster job start, better customer experience
- Stronger documentation for insurance claims

---

### 5. Work Authorizations
The AI automatically sends a digital work authorization to the customer immediately after intake. By the time the tech arrives, the signature is done.

- Sent via SMS and/or email automatically — no manual step
- Custom authorization contract templates (company-specific language)
- Customer signs on their own device
- Signature stored to the job record
- Locks the job in before a competitor can call back

---

### 6. Team Management
One dashboard for the entire crew's availability, certifications, and service areas.

- Live availability view (who's working, who's off, where they are)
- Skill-based routing by certification type: Water, Fire, Mold, or custom
- Multi-office / territory management: admins set specific zip codes and service areas per branch
- Full calendar sync for all appointments and dispatches

---

### 7. Dashboard & Analytics
Complete visibility into every call, lead, and dollar.

- Click any call to: listen to the audio, read the AI-generated summary, read the full transcript
- **Leaky Bucket Prevention:** Incomplete or unresolved calls are flagged as "Unreviewed Leads" — a daily review queue to ensure nothing falls through
- Auto-tagging: calls labeled by outcome (e.g., "Lead Captured - Emergency," "Lead Captured - Inspection," "Disqualified - Out of Area")
- Track calls, bookings, disqualifications, and revenue over time

---

### 8. Review Tool & Post-Job Follow-Up
Automated reputation management and customer follow-up from job completion onward.

- **Smart Review Requests:** AI sends personalized SMS review requests with custom AI-generated images (e.g., a photo of the company van with "Hey [Customer First Name]!" overlay)
- **Review Filtering:** Intercepts negative feedback — good ratings go to Google, bad ratings go to an internal feedback form
- **Automated Communication Timeline:**
  - Instant SMS appointment confirmation
  - 24-hour reminder
  - 1-hour reminder
  - Post-job review request

---

### Bonus: Website Voice Chat Widget
A widget that embeds on the company's website and lets visitors instantly speak with the AI dispatcher by voice — not just chat. Same AI, same intake logic, live on the website.

## Content & Marketing Notes

### Why restoration owners are hard to convert
Many restoration contractors don't have high call volume — they rely on insurance adjuster referrals, not inbound calls. For these companies, the missed-call pain point is abstract. Better conversion angle for this segment: **the storm surge play** (when volume does spike, you're the one who answers) and **the referral network angle** (adjusters track response times and quietly deprioritize slow responders).

### The expansion opportunity
The platform works for any on-site service business. Adjacent verticals with strong fit:
- Duct cleaning (proven — case study above)
- HVAC emergency service
- Plumbing (emergency and scheduled)
- Carpet cleaning (custom pricing feature is a natural fit)
- Biohazard / trauma cleanup
- Roofing (emergency tarping during storm events)
- Appliance repair
- Pest control

**Content strategy:** Blog posts and SEO content can target these verticals without changing the website's restoration-first positioning. Each post establishes authority in a new vertical while linking back to the core product.

### Brand voice
- Direct, no fluff
- Restoration industry language: IICRC, FNOL, mitigation, adjuster, preferred vendor, work authorization, Cat 1/2/3
- Founder story: Santino Velci, from Hawaii, built after uncle lost $80k condo job to a missed call
- Metric-driven: always specific numbers, never vague claims
